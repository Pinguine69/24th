Admissions

Entry Requirements

We review recent school reports, predicted grades and a short conversation with each applicant to confirm the right course level.

  • GCSE programmes typically require evidence of working at Grade 4 or above in core subjects.
  • A-Level entry is normally based on at least five GCSEs at Grade 5+, including the subjects you plan to continue.
  • We welcome contextual information and design transition support where additional stretch or catch-up is needed.

How to Apply

Follow the three-step timeline below. Our Admissions Team will guide you through each stage.

1) Enquire

Tell us about your goals. We'll guide you on suitable pathways and availability.

2) Meet & Assess

An interview and placement assessment ensure the right programme and support plan.

3) Offer & Enrol

Receive your offer, confirm your place and get your personalised timetable.

Admissions FAQs

What are the entry requirements?

Entry requirements vary by programme. We review recent reports and, where helpful, a short placement assessment to ensure the right course level. Please see Entry Requirements or contact Admissions.

How do I apply?

Submit an enquiry or complete the online application form on our Contact & Apply page. We will then arrange an interview and advise on next steps.

Do you accept international students?

Yes. We welcome international applicants and can advise on visa routes and documentation. See International Students for guidance.

Can I retake exams or study part-time?

We offer flexible part-time and retake options alongside full-time GCSE and A-Level courses. We will tailor a timetable to your needs.

Are scholarships or bursaries available?

Financial support may be available depending on circumstances. Please contact the Admissions team for current fees, scholarships and bursary information.
