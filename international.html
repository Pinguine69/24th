International Students

We hold Highly Trusted Sponsor status with the UK Home Office and regularly welcome students relocating to Croydon from across the world.

Overview & Aims

We are a highly trusted sponsor authorised by the UK Home Office and we welcome learners from around the globe. Our aim is to help every international student gain qualifications that are recognised and valued by universities and employers worldwide.

  • Highly Trusted Sponsor status with the UK Home Office.
  • Programmes that lead to established UK academic and professional awards.
  • Tailored guidance to help students settle quickly into college and London life.

Arrival Support

Our airport meet-and-greet service ensures you are welcomed the moment you land. Simply share your confirmed flight itinerary and we will arrange transport to the college or your accommodation.

  • Escorted transfers from London airports to the college or your chosen address.
  • Advance coordination with families or guardians to confirm timings.
  • Service availability subject to booking confirmation and an additional fee.

Working in London

Your ability to work depends on the type of immigration permission you hold. We help you interpret the rules so that you remain fully compliant.

  • Most students may work limited hours provided work placements do not exceed 50% of their course.
  • Working for yourself, running a business, professional sport or entertainment roles, and permanent full-time posts are not permitted.
  • If your immigration permission excludes work, you cannot accept paid or unpaid roles, including course-related placements.

After your studies you may be eligible to remain in the UK to work under specific government schemes. Always check the latest guidance from the UK Visas and Immigration service.

Visa Requirements

We support Child Student and Student visa applications and advise on the evidence you need to provide.

Who can apply?

  • Students aged 16 or above.
  • Applicants studying at Level 3 or higher (equivalent to UK Foundation or first year of a degree).

How long can you stay?

  • For courses of 12 months or longer you can receive the full course length plus up to four additional months.
  • For courses below degree level (such as HNDs or certificates) visas are normally granted for up to three years plus four additional months.

What evidence do you need?

  • 40 points under the UK points-based system: 30 points for enrolment with Croydon Metropolitan College (a licensed Tier 4 provider) and 10 points for demonstrating sufficient funds.
  • Supporting documents including passport, academic transcripts and, where applicable, consent letters for students under 18.

International FAQs

Can I work while studying?

Visa conditions vary. Many students aged 16+ can work limited hours in term time. Always check the allowance on your BRP and contact us if you are unsure.

Do you arrange guardianship?

Parents/guardians must appoint an authorised UK guardian for learners under 18. We can connect you with reputable guardianship agencies if required.

How early should I apply?

We recommend submitting applications at least one term before your intended start date to allow time for visa processing and travel arrangements.
