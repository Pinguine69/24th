GCSE Mathematics

The new 9-1 GCSE Maths course is designed to stretch learners across a wide range of problem-solving contexts. Students learn not only how to tackle calculations, but how to evaluate strategies and select the most efficient method for the problem they face. With fewer formulae provided in exams and synoptic questions that draw on multiple areas of mathematics, the course demands deeper understanding and confidence.

GCSE Maths is a core requirement for further study, university entry and many professional pathways. As the grading scale pushes learners towards higher levels of challenge, CMC supports every student to move up through the grades with security and self-belief.

Course Structure

The specification is organised into six broad topic areas that are revisited throughout the two-year programme:

  • Number
  • Algebra
  • Geometry
  • Proportion
  • Measure
  • Statistics (Probability and Data Handling)

Teaching is thematic and synoptic-topics are brought together so that students apply knowledge in real-life contexts such as physics, finance and data science. Significant time is devoted to reasoning, modelling and developing flexible problem-solving skills.

Assessment

Assessment comprises three equally weighted papers covering the full range of content. Paper 1 is non-calculator, while Papers 2 and 3 require a scientific calculator. Each paper begins with accessible (grade 1) questions and quickly moves into more demanding problems targeting grade 9.

Grading Tiers

  • Higher Tier: Grades 4-9
  • Foundation Tier: Grades 1-6
